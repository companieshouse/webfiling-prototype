During this confirmation period, were any of the company’s shares admitted to trading on a market?

What does trading on a market mean?
Trading shares on a market makes a company a 'traded company'. This means that during the confirmation period, the company had shares admitted to one or more of the following:

  • a market established under the rules of a UK recognised investment exchange
  • any other regulated market in or outside of the UK
  • any other market outside of the UK

The term ‘traded company’ should not be confused with ‘trading company’, which means a company that is not dormant).
